MeowMan1984 03/22/2023 5:48 AM
Hi @everyone We reached out to the team at Forgotten Animals and got some insight to what is going on. There is a two part update (I won't ping the second; just read from here) and some photo's they've sent through. To be honest, the update and photos are a bit of a tear jerker. Knowing we've played a role in helping these people is deeply moving and makes me so glad we're doing what we're doing. I hope it warms your heart as much as it does mine.
Dear Andrew and the Meowcoin team,
>
Thanks to the continuous support of the amazing crypto community, we are able to keep our work in Ukraine supporting volunteers and shelters in destroyed and occupied cities, making sure thousands of animals are rescued, fed, treated, neutered, cared for and many rehomed, allowing volunteers to rescue more in their place. We were also involved in providing emergency relief to animals after the Turkey earthquake through one of our volunteers currently based there and working directly on the ground.
>
As the public interest toward the situation in Ukraine sadly wanes, the country's animal welfare activists, more than ever, are in dire need of any support to come their way – the number of abandoned animals and pets who lost their owners and their homes is huge and is growing every day. Over the past months, our focus remained on providing free neutering services across Ukraine in cities like Sumy, Belopolye , Kramatorsk Dnipro, Kherson and others, which tremendously alleviates the workload for volunteers, as there is not enough food to feed all the animals.
>
Since the last update in November, with your overall contribution of 3000 USD we were able to neuter about 80 animals (the average cost of neutering is 25 USD), thus preventing thousands of kittens and puppies from being born to suffer and have nearly zero chance at a safe life. 500 USD was spent on emergency food and vet supplies for animals during the Turkey earthquake. The rest of funds went into providing emergency food for animals kept in shelters and those cared for by the brave volunteers, who stayed behind despite the war to help animals in need.
>
Overall, since the war began in February 2022, we sterilized over 1550 cats and dogs and counting – we had to reduce the number of cities we can work in now, due to drastically reduced funding, but our campaign is currently active in 3 cities across Ukraine and we can keep adding locations if the funding and the veterinarian services availability allow it.
>
Please see attached some pictures of the animals we've helped thanks to your support, below are their stories. We are very grateful to the crypto community for their generosity.

1) Turkey Last month Turkey and Syria experienced massive earthquakes that destroyed thousands of buildings and left around 50,000 people dead and more injured. Devastated houses and displaced families mean thousands of abandoned animals, and, unfortunately, they are often the last to be rescued, we try to do our best with the resources we have and help as many as we can. Being on a mission to provide help where it’s most urgent, we teamed up with a local group of volunteers in Turkey that provided immediate response to the remote villages in the Kahramanmaraş province - where the aid doesn't often reach as quickly as it's needed. They delivered food, medicine, and other necessary animal supplies, as well as transportation to evacuate animals to safer locations.
>
2) Mariupol, Ukraine Anzhelika from Mariupol is one of those "invisible heroes" who save animals abandoned due to the war. Now she's taking care of more than 30 cats. Some of them were supposed to be given to foster families but didn't find their homes due to the war. But most of the cats she saved after the war started. During the military operations in the city, Anzhelika had to wander around destroyed storages to find food for herself and her animals. If she saw another abandoned cat or a dog, she would take them home. Anzhelika would have already left ruined Mariupol, but her animals held her back because she would never abandon them. Besides looking after and feeding her animals, Anzhelika is committed to treating, vaccinating and spaying and neutering them, all of which we helped with, so that Anzhelika can save more animals in need.
>
3) Moldova the war in Ukraine isn’t only hard on Ukrainians, the nearby counties are taking a hit, having to spread already limited resources to try and help millions of refugees running for their lives. Natalia from Moldova, Europe’s poorest country, cannot be defined as anything but a superhero. She rescued, sterilized and rehomed over 900 cats and many dogs, using only her very strained resources, animals of Moldova are simply lucky to have Natalia in their corner, their before and after pictures speak for themselves. We helped Natalia with sterilization of her rescued animals, so that she can find them a new home and take in more abandoned animals in need.
