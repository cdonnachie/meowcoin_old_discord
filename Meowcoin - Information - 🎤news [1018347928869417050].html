UPDATE FROM FORGOTTEN ANIMALS Hi @everyone. Please read below from the Forgotten Animals team. A great way to see the real world impact you're having
Dear Andrew and the Meow Coin community, thanks to the support of the amazing crypto community we are able to continue our work in Ukraine helping volunteers and shelters in destroyed and occupied cities and making sure thousands of animals are rescued, fed, treated, neutered and cared for. Our main focus remains on providing free neutering services across Ukraine in cities like Kharkiv, Kiyv, Dnipro, Kherson, Mariupol and many others, which greatly alleviates the workload for volunteers, as there is simply not enough food to feed all the animals abandoned because of war. With your latest contribution we're able to neuter about 20 animals (the average cost of neutering is 25 USD), thus preventing thousands from being born to suffer and provide emergency food for animals at shelters and cared for by the brave volunteers, who stayed in Ukraine despite war to help animals in need, as many lose their owners or get abandoned. Most charities working in Ukraine are now focusing on rescuing, feeding and relocating animals, all while unneutered animals keep adding litters to the already huge army of strays suffering and dying on the streets and making the work of volunteers, who are already stretched thin and swamped with work, even harder. We are the only organisation providing neutering in Mariupol and Kherson, cities no foreign charity would work in as it's a recently occupied Russian territory. There are still over 100.000 people in Mariupol and thousands of animals. People and animals, who have to fault they are under occupation. It doesn't mean they don't need our help anymore. In general, since the war began in February 2022, we sterilized over 1200 cats and dogs and counting, our campaign is currently active in 10+ cities across Ukraine and we keep adding locations as the funding and the veterinarian services availability allow us to. See attached some pictures of the animals we've helped thanks to your support, below are their stories. We are very grateful to the crypto community for their generosity. pic 1. Lana, the dog in the photo is a survivor of ongoing shelling in Belopolye, Sumy region of Ukraine. Lana and her pup were saved by Ukrainian soldiers after the house of Victor, their owner, was destroyed by bombing and Victor was killed. The puppy was named Mavka and got a collar with the Armed Forces of Ukraine emblem. After a few days, Mavka was killed by a shell fragment…The story of Lana is incredibly sad but has a happy ending. She was lucky to survive and found a new loving owner (on the photo) thanks our volunteers, the was spayed through our free spaying campaign, so that she never has to give birth to unwanted puppies. pic 2,3 and 4. Oleg (on the photo with a kitten he rescued, among hundreds of other animals), is just an incredible human being. Living in Zaporizzhia, Ukraine, Oleg was staying at a make-shift tent camp in the woods where he was living with and feeding all the animals he rescued, before and during the war. The place was not fit for proper housing and treatment of dozens of animals currently under his care. Yet, despite his own precarious conditions, Oleg constantly helps the elderly and the poor residents in his aread by neutering and fostering their pets. After the war started, Oleg moved to an unfinished house abandoned by its owner, that will now be used as a shelter for his animals as a more spacious, comfortable, and warm place to accommodate and foster more rescue dogs and cats. Thanks to your donation we helped Oleg neutere and feed his residents. This Christmas we will be doing a donation drive in order to help Oleg finish building some parts ot the house in order to save even more animals affected by the war.
